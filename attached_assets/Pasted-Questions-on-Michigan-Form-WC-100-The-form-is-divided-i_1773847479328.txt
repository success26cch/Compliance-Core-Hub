Questions on Michigan Form WC-100
The form is divided into sections covering the employee, employer, and the incident itself. 
I. Employee Data
Personal Identification: Social Security Number, Name (Last, First, MI), and Home Address.
Demographics: Date of Birth, Sex, and Telephone Number.
Dependents & Tax Info: Number of Dependents and Tax Filing Status (Single, Head of Household, Married Filing Joint, or Married Filing Separate).

II. Employer/Carrier Data
Business Details: Employer Name, Street Address, Federal ID Number (FEIN), and UI (Unemployment Insurance) number.
Categorization: Type of Business (SIC/NAICS code) and Injury Location Code.
Insurance Info: Insurance Company Name and Telephone Number.

III. Injury/Medical Data
Timing: Date of injury, last day worked, date of return to work (if applicable), and date of death (if applicable).
Incident Details:
Time employee began work and time of the event.
Specific location (City, State, County) and whether it occurred on employer premises.
"What was the employee doing just before the incident occurred?" (Describe activity, tools, and materials).
"How did the injury occur?" (Detailed step-by-step account).
"What object or substance directly harmed the employee?" (e.g., concrete floor, chlorine).
The Injury: Description of the nature of the injury and the specific part of the body affected.
Medical Treatment:
Name of physician or healthcare professional.
Whether the employee was treated in an ER or hospitalized overnight.
Location of treatment if given away from the worksite.

IV. Occupation and Wage Data
Employment History: Date of hire and specific occupation.
Wages: Total gross weekly wage (highest 39 of the last 52 weeks) and number of weeks used.
Other Benefits: Value of discontinued fringe benefits.
Worker Status: Was the employee a volunteer or certified as vocationally handicapped?.
Reporting: Date the employer was notified by the employee.

What Workers' Comp Adjusters Need to Know 
Adjusters use this information to determine compensability (whether the claim is covered) and calculate benefits. Specifically, they look for: 
Employers
Employers
Causation: Clear evidence that the injury "arose out of and in the course of employment".
Notification Timelines: Proof the employee reported the injury promptly to the employer.
Medical Substantiation: Details of the first treatment to match the injury description with medical findings.
Average Weekly Wage: Critical for calculating the correct disability payment amount.
