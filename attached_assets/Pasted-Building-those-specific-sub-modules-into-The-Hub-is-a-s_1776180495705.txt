Building those specific sub-modules into The Hub is a smart move. It transforms the platform from a "safety tool" into a comprehensive "Environmental, Health, and Safety" (EHS) command center.

Since Corey is your regulatory expert, these modules should be structured around the CFR 40 (EPA) requirements. Here is a breakdown of how to build those sub-modules to ensure they are audit-ready and functional:

1. Air (Clean Air Act - CAA)
This is often the most complex area due to permitting and emissions tracking.

Permit Management: A "Digital Filing Cabinet" for Title V or Minor Source permits, with automated alerts for renewal dates.

Emissions Calculator: A tool for users to input run-times or fuel usage to estimate VOCs, HAPs, or Greenhouse Gases.

Visible Emissions (Method 9) Log: A simple mobile-friendly form for "Opacity" checks if the facility has stacks or dust collectors.

2. Waste (RCRA - Resource Conservation & Recovery Act)
Focus on the "Cradle-to-Grave" tracking that auditors look for.

Satellite Accumulation Point (SAP) Tracker: A digital log for weekly container inspections.

Manifest Manager: A place to upload and link Hazardous Waste manifests. It should flag any manifest that hasn't been "returned/signed" by the TSDF within 45 days.

Generator Status Calculator: Automatically determine if they are a Very Small (VSQG), Small (SQG), or Large Quantity Generator (LQG) based on monthly waste logs.

3. SPCC (Spill Prevention, Control, and Countermeasure)
This is high-stakes for any facility with significant oil or fuel storage.

Tank & Container Inspections: Scheduled checklists (monthly/annual) specifically for secondary containment integrity.

Spill Kit Inventory: A quick QR-code scan to verify spill kits are stocked and unblocked.

Plan Review Trigger: A reminder to review and re-certify the SPCC plan every 5 years (or whenever a major change occurs).

4. Stormwater (NPDES / SWPPP)
Focus on the "industrial activity exposed to rain" aspect.

Visual Monitoring Logs: A module for the "Quarterly Visual Assessment" of stormwater outfalls during rain events.

SWPPP Action Tracker: If a "hit" or high concentration is found in sampling, this triggers a corrective action workflow to fix the source of the pollution.

BMP Maintenance: A schedule for cleaning out catch basins or replacing silt socks.

5. Universal Waste & Special Waste
This is a "catch-all" for common items that are technically hazardous but managed under streamlined rules.

Streamlined Categories: Separate logs for Batteries, Lamps (Bulbs), Pesticides, and Mercury-containing equipment.

Storage Time Tracker: Universal waste can usually only be stored for one year. The Hub should have a "countdown" for each container once the first item is placed in it.

The "Corey" Integration Strategy
To make these modules stand out, you can have Corey act as the "Intelligence Layer":

Regulatory Alerts: When the EPA updates a rule (like the recent PFOA/PFAS rulings), Corey can send a "Compliance Bulletin" directly to the user's dashboard.

The "Ask Corey" Field: Within the Waste module, a user could ask, "Corey, how should I label a 55-gallon drum of used coolant in Michigan?" and get an instant, regulation-backed answer.

One tip for the "Universal Waste" module: Many clients forget that Aerosol Cans were added to the federal Universal Waste list recently. Adding a specific sub-category for aerosols would be a huge "win" for your users.
